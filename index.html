About Wales

Wales is a country in the United Kingdom, it has a rugged coastline, mountainous national parks, lots of castles, and the Celtic culture of the people. In the northwest region of the country there is Snowdonia mountain and its national park.

The 2016 population was estimated to be about 3.168 million people, 96% are White British, 2.1% are non-white. There is more than 1680 miles of coastline and is hilly. The majority of higher crests are in the central areas and the north zones of Wales. Wales is viewed as a modern day Celtic country which shows many people still hold on to the traditions of the Celts.

Some facts about Wales

Just 21% of the population can talk the local dialect, all the statues in the capital city of Cardiff are of animals and Marconi's initial radio transmission was between two different points in Wales; these were Lavernock Isle and three miles away in Flat Holm.

The national anthem of Wales is Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau which translated means the land of my fathers which is the newest anthem after God Bless the Prince of Wales
